Equipment Needed:
-A cricket ball and bat/gears
-Stumps (3 vertical sticks) and bails (2 small sticks on top)
-A field (usually oval) with a 22-yard pitch in the center

Roles:
Each team gets to bat and bowl/field per game and each team has 11 players.
-Batting Team: Scores runs
-Bowling/Fielding Team: Tries to limit runs and dismiss the batsmen

Starting the Game:
-Toss: Captains of both teams flip a coin to decide which team will bat or bowl first.
-Setting Up the Pitch: Place stumps on both ends of the 22-yard pitch.

Playing the Game:
-Bowling:
 A bowler delivers the ball from one end of the pitch to the batsman at the other.
 The bowler must bowl overarm and keep their front foot behind the crease line.

-Batting:
 The batsman tries to hit the ball and score runs
   -Runs are scored by running between the wickets or by hitting the ball to the boundary:
   -4 runs if the ball rolls to the boundary.
   -6 runs if the ball clears the boundary without touching the ground.

-Fielding:
Fielders stop the ball, catch it, or throw it to hit the stumps to get the batsman out.

-Ways To Get Out:
 Bowled: The ball hits the stumps and dislodges the bails.

 Caught: A fielder catches the ball before it touches the ground.

 Run Out: A fielder hits the stumps while the batsman is running between the wickets.

 LBW (Leg Before Wicket): The ball hits the batsman's leg in line with the stumps.

 Stumped: The wicketkeeper dislodges the bails when the batsman is outside the crease.

Scoring and Winning:
-Innings: Each team gets one or two turns (innings) to bat, depending on the format (Test, ODI, or T20).

-Run Target: The team batting second tries to score more runs than the first team.

-Win Conditions: Team with the most runs wins.
 In cricket, a tie may occur if both teams score the same runs then they will go to a super over, where each team has to score the most amount of runs in one over (6 balls).

Formats of Cricket:
-Test Match: Played over five days with two innings per team.

-One Day International (ODI): 50 overs (300 balls) per team.
-T20: 20 overs (120 balls) per team.
